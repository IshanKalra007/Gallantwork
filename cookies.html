1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. They help the website:

  • Function correctly
  • Remember your preferences
  • Improve performance
  • Understand how the site is used

2. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies to:

  • Make our website work properly
  • Improve security and performance
  • Understand how visitors use our website
  • Improve user experience

3. Types of Cookies We Use

A. Strictly Necessary Cookies (Always On)

These are essential for the website to function and cannot be switched off.

Examples:

  • Security cookies
  • Session management
  • Cookie consent preference storage

Legal basis: Legitimate interest / required for service

B. Analytics / Performance Cookies (Optional)

These help us understand how visitors use the website (e.g., page visits, traffic sources).

These cookies are only used if you give consent.

Examples:

  • Google Analytics
  • Azure Application Insights
  • Microsoft Clarity

C. Functional Cookies (Optional)

These remember your preferences and choices.

Used only with consent.

D. Marketing Cookies (Optional)

These may track visitors across websites to show relevant advertising.

Used only with explicit consent.

4. Cookie Consent

When you first visit our website, you will see a cookie banner allowing you to:

  • Accept all cookies
  • Reject all non-essential cookies
  • Manage your preferences

We do not place non-essential cookies unless you consent.

You can change your preferences at any time using the Cookie Settings link on our website.

5. Third-Party Cookies

Some cookies may be set by third-party services we use (e.g., analytics providers).

These providers may process data outside the UK. Where this happens, appropriate safeguards are used.
